# ANTI_PATTERNS.md

# 1. PURPOSE

This document defines **what the project must NOT become**.

It exists to prevent:

- product drift
- over-engineering
- loss of identity
- cultural degradation

---

# 2. CRITICAL ANTI-PATTERNS

## 2.1 Crypto-First UX

❌ Showing:
- wallets
- tokens
- gas fees

Why it's bad:
- breaks UX
- alienates mainstream users

Correct approach:
→ blockchain must be invisible

---

## 2.2 Algorithm-Only Discovery

❌ Relying only on ML recommendations

Why it's bad:
- homogenizes music
- removes culture
- creates bubbles

Correct approach:
→ 4-engine discovery system

---

## 2.3 AI Content Flood

❌ Allowing mass AI-generated uploads

Why it's bad:
- destroys trust
- kills discovery quality
- devalues human artists

Correct approach:
→ human verification + limits + curation

---

## 2.4 Over-Tokenization

❌ Turning everything into NFTs

Why it's bad:
- unnecessary complexity
- speculative behavior
- UX degradation

Correct approach:
→ tokens only where they add real value

---

## 2.5 Social Media Drift

❌ Becoming:
- a feed
- a content addiction machine

Why it's bad:
- distracts from music
- reduces cultural depth

Correct approach:
→ music-first experience

---

## 2.6 Over-Engineering

❌ Building:
- too many smart contracts
- unnecessary decentralization

Why it's bad:
- slower development
- higher costs
- harder to maintain

Correct approach:
→ minimal viable architecture

---

## 2.7 Payola / Hidden Promotion

❌ Hidden paid promotion

Why it's bad:
- destroys trust
- corrupts discovery

Correct approach:
→ transparent sponsored discovery

---

## 2.8 Ignoring Curators

❌ Not incentivizing human taste

Why it's bad:
- weak discovery
- no cultural layer

Correct approach:
→ curator economy

---

## 2.9 Pure Data-Driven Culture

❌ Only optimizing for:
- clicks
- streams
- retention

Why it's bad:
- kills artistic diversity

Correct approach:
→ include cultural signals

---

## 2.10 Breaking Simplicity

❌ Complex UI / too many features

Why it's bad:
- reduces adoption
- increases friction

Correct approach:
→ minimal, fast, intuitive UX

---

# 3. WARNING SIGNALS

If any of these appear → STOP and reassess:

- users need explanation to use core features
- artists don’t understand payouts
- discovery feels repetitive
- too many low-quality tracks
- UX feels like a crypto app
- curators lose relevance
